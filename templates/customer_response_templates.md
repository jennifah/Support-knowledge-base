# 💬 Customer Response Templates

**Purpose:** Consistent, professional, empathetic responses for common support scenarios.  
**How to use:** Copy the template, fill in the `[brackets]`, personalise the tone.  
**Rule:** Never send a template without reading it fully first.

---

## 1. Acknowledging a Complex Issue

Use when: The issue will take time to investigate and you cannot resolve immediately.

---

Hi [Customer Name],

Thank you for reaching out — I can see why this is frustrating, and I want to make sure we get to the bottom of it properly.

I've reviewed what you've shared and I'm looking into [brief description of the issue] now. This will require me to [check our logs / speak with our Engineering team / reproduce the issue on my end], so I want to set the right expectation: I'll have a full update for you by [specific time, e.g. end of day tomorrow].

In the meantime, could you confirm:
- [Specific question 1]
- [Specific question 2]

This will help me move faster on your behalf.

I'll be in touch shortly.

[Your name]  
Support Team

---

## 2. Resolving an Integration Issue

Use when: You've identified the root cause and have a fix or workaround.

---

Hi [Customer Name],

Good news — I've identified what's causing the issue with [integration name].

**What was happening:**  
[Plain-language explanation of the root cause. No jargon. E.g. "The webhook was subscribed to the wrong event type, which meant it was listening for events that never triggered in your workflow."]

**What I've done / What you need to do:**  
[Step-by-step fix. Number each step if there are more than two.]

1. [Step 1]
2. [Step 2]

**To confirm it's working:**  
[Tell them exactly what to check, e.g. "You should see a new entry in your delivery logs within 5 minutes of your next candidate being added."]

If anything doesn't look right after following these steps, reply here and I'll pick it up straight away.

[Your name]  
Support Team

---

## 3. Escalation Update (Keeping Customer Informed)

Use when: Issue is with Engineering and you have no resolution yet.

---

Hi [Customer Name],

I wanted to check in with a quick update on [issue description].

Our Engineering team is actively investigating. Here's where we are:

- **What we know:** [What has been confirmed so far]
- **What we're doing:** [What Engineering is currently working on]
- **Next update:** I'll follow up by [specific time]

I know waiting is frustrating, and I appreciate your patience. This ticket is a priority and I'm tracking it closely on your behalf.

[Your name]  
Support Team

---

## 4. Closing a Resolved Ticket

Use when: Issue is fully resolved and you're closing the ticket.

---

Hi [Customer Name],

I'm glad we were able to get this sorted for you.

To summarise what happened and what we did:

- **Issue:** [One sentence]
- **Root cause:** [One sentence]
- **Resolution:** [One sentence]

If you run into anything else or notice anything unusual, don't hesitate to reach out — I'm happy to help.

[Your name]  
Support Team

---

## 5. Declining a Feature Request (Empathetically)

Use when: A customer requests a feature you cannot deliver or is not on the roadmap.

---

Hi [Customer Name],

Thank you for sharing this — feedback like this is genuinely valuable and goes directly to our Product team.

I want to be upfront with you: [feature request] isn't something we're able to offer right now. I don't want to over-promise a timeline, so rather than say "it's coming soon," I'd rather be honest that it's not currently on our near-term roadmap.

What I can suggest in the meantime: [workaround or alternative approach, if one exists].

I've logged your feedback with the details of your use case, which is the most useful thing I can do to advocate for this being prioritised in future.

Thank you again for taking the time to share this.

[Your name]  
Support Team
